About NASA

NASA explores the unknown in air and space, leading the United States civil space program and aeronautics research. From studying Earth’s climate to probing distant galaxies, NASA’s missions seek to expand human knowledge and answer fundamental questions about our universe. Working with commercial and international partners, the agency operates spacecraft, telescopes, and the International Space Station to conduct cutting‑edge science and technology demonstrations. NASA also develops advanced aviation and spaceflight technologies that make air travel safer, more efficient, and more sustainable. Through education, outreach, and open data, NASA inspires students, innovators, and explorers around the world to participate in the journey of discovery.
